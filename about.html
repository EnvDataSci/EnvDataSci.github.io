About Environment Data Science

Bio

I am a PhD student at the University of Edinburgh School of GeoSciences. I research Scottish mountain snow and particularly how it relates to flooding. You can read more about my research on my blog, scottishsnow. Prior to my return to university I worked as a hydrologist for Environment Agency Wales and Halcrow. I worked on projects including river and coastal flood mapping, flood defence scheme design, river reallignment, flood forecasting (operationally and model development) and flood risk assessment.

Key skills

  • R programming and data analysis
  • GIS analysis (QGIS, GRASS, MapInfo and ArcGIS)
  • Hydrological consultancy
  • Database design and queries (SQLite)
