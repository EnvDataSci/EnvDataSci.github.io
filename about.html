About Environment Data Science

Bio

Environment Data Science is run by Mike Spencer. He is a PhD student at the University of Edinburgh School of GeoSciences. Mike researches Scottish mountain snow and particularly how it relates to flooding. You can read more about Mike's research on his blog, scottishsnow.

Prior to Mike's return to university he worked as a hydrologist for Environment Agency Wales and Halcrow. Mike worked on projects including river and coastal flood mapping, flood defence scheme design, river reallignment, flood forecasting (operationally and model development) and flood risk assessment. Mike graduated from Lancaster University in 2004 with a BSc in Environmental Science.

Key skills

  • R programming and data analysis
  • GIS analysis (QGIS, GRASS, MapInfo and ArcGIS)
  • Hydrological consultancy
  • Database design and queries (SQLite)
  • Training and development
  • Open source software
